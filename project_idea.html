Yes Chef!
An Application for The Hospitality Industry


Overview:

I intend to create application that will have a business component (food costing, recipes, daily invoicing and wage control) and a
social aspect which will allow chefs engage with each other and share ideas.
The app will take input from the user in the form of goods, operating percentages, profits & expenses to allow a day-to-day
feedback on where the business sits both financially and stock wise.
The application will also delve into rostering, ordering and very importantly learning / teaching.
Yes Chef! will be the go-to application for the Hospitality industry.

Motivation:

As of June 2016, there were a little over 85,000 eating out establishments in Australia with only 34% of these
belonging to the Fast food industry, yet there is a complete lack of an application that tackles the behind the
scenes of kitchen operation. I have encountered these challenges in my previous roles s Head Chef and have
created workaround spreadsheets using Microsoft Excel and processes which lead me to conclude that is a gap in
the market which this app may fill.
This app will not only save a busy chef time but will also streamline processes for the payroll and
accounts department. As a retired chef moving into the IT profession, it is my goal to create this
application and join my two passions passing on knowledge to new generations of Chefs.

Description:

The completed application will consist of many different elements that chefs deal with on a regular basis.


Dish Costing:
A page that will let the chef add ingredients, ingredients cost, & the percentage that the
business expects to make from food sales. This will then output the sale price for the chef and
create a printable clean recipe card for the rest of the team to follow.


Invoice Control:
A page that would have drop down menus for each supplier, item & amount as well as a
search bar for quick referencing. The page would store every item that the kitchen buys much
like a spreadsheet, this list can be added to during invoice entry, dish costing or from a
shopping list with the potential to have APIs for suppliers to directly submit invoice information
saving the chef time.


Cost Monitoring by the item:
In this area, I will show every purchased item throughout a selected period that
has had a price increase or decrease, each change will be highlighted to the day so that the
Chef can pinpoint when a supplier has changed a price and address the issue if necessary.


Ordering:
Here the Chef can create an order list or shopping list. Each item can be designated
to a preferred supplier, the Chef can then phone, email or app order knowing that the items are
being sent to the correct supplier, API’s can once again be used to directly order from suppliers
that have an online system.


Rostering:
Rostering will let the chef create and cost a working roster it will have drag and drop features
as shifts in the industry change regularly. The roster will also be able to be sent directly to all
team members.


The recipe book:
This is a both online & on device area where the Chef can store & share recipes, the
share side being optional as some recipes are considered “top secret”. Upon importing a recipe
new items can be added to the shopping list and suggest a local supplier for this ingredient.
This section will offer the user the ability to customize their preferred supplier.


The Group:
A small social media section which will offer capacity to add industry-based contacts and
different information shared, for example recipes or supplier contacts. This would also have
the potential to be expanded to include job advertising and searching within a specified area.


Learning:
A help station for new users to familiarize and use the application & informal mentoring and
support for both employees who are new to leadership roles in the kitchen and those already in leadership positions.


Welcome Page:
This is the landing page which will contain notifications and links to all items within the app.


Running Tally:
This will be the master sheet, a daily, weekly, monthly & yearly running tally of all cost and
expenditure, the user will be able to see wages, food cost and revenue all together and break
down each item as they need to see it.


All these pages will be available in both an online (Web) format and mobile app format.


Tools & Technologies:

Programming and application development tools such as Python & Xamarin will be essential for development,
although at this stage, I am unsure exactly which software I will use. Test hardware in the form of an iPad,
iPhone, Android device & computer (low end spec) to make sure anyone can run the software. And finally,
a finished product can shipped preinstalled and set up on a device selected by the client. This would obviously
incur an additional cost or contract on the device.


Skills Required:

Programming & Design, are the 2 key skills that will be needed for this application. The user interface will need
to be simple to use, easy to navigate and quick in transitions between pages or sections. Communication will
also be a major factor as concepts move between the two industries (Hospitality & IT). I am currently studying
programming as well as IT and plan on shaping my degree to obtain as many skills as I can in Web
development & Application development.


Outcome:

Upon successful completion of the application, I would have a fully functional Hospitality application that can
serve not only Chefs and kitchen staff but also other areas like Bars and housekeeping.
The application which is targeted at the Hospitality industry is a result of my passion for what I have done
over the last 20 years. It will be an information repository for future chefs and a learning tool for those
who are new to management roles within the Hospitality industry and a financial tool for managing a kitchen budget.
This application will be the bridge between Hospitality & Information Technology
