Interest in IT:

During high school, I was introduced to using computers for minor tasks in class.
I remember being interested in using MS Paint to determine its functionality.
I was also interested in playing small basic games on the computers.
The early interest that I had in IT was continually fed by my high school IT teacher,
Mr Wilks who would engage in conversation for hours about technology, computers and software programs
both during class, break time and outside of school time.

My passion for IT escalated throughout the 90’s, as I was exposed to using PC’s for
gaming and other media editing and creating tasks.
I was in awe of local studios like Westwood for their production of intricate PC games
like Dune 2000 & Command and Conquer, which were hard to comprehend that they were the product of a
few people using a PC rather than hundreds of people behind the scenes.

Into the 2000’s, I moved to Australia and my main hobby was now tinkering with computers,
trying to run high end games and saving pennies to buy the next piece of hardware to upgrade.
By this stage I had also had a good understanding of operating systems like Windows, MSDOS and some Linux software.
In 2020, I opened my own small business to provide people in my local area with an affordable basic tech
support and servicing which has a significant waiting time at the local computer businesses.
This brings us to today, I have chosen RMIT as the next building block in my career in IT.
The university has a fantastic reputation and offers a learning structure that fits my lifestyle and
learning needs as a remote student. I hope RMIT will take me out of my comfort zone and open new avenues for me within the IT world.
I am aware that for the most part, I have always worked on computer hardware and not delved too far into
much of anything else.
I expect that through my studies, I will become familiar with the software applications,
programming and information security as we as concepts that I not yet thought about.
